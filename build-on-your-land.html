Common Questions

Questions Landowners Ask Us

Buildable land needs to meet several conditions: appropriate zoning for residential use, adequate soil for septic (if not on city sewer), access to water (well or municipal), and physical characteristics that allow for a foundation — no severe flooding, excessive slope, or protected wetlands. Topography, setback requirements, and utility availability all factor in. That's why we start with a land assessment before any other planning.

At minimum, you'll need a building permit from your county. Depending on your situation, you may also need:

  • Septic system permit from your county Health Department
  • Well permit (some counties require this before drilling)
  • Electrical permit and HVAC permit
  • Driveway access permit from your county road department
  • Grading or stormwater permit if your site requires significant earth movement

Requirements vary county by county in Central Kentucky. We coordinate all of this as part of the process.

From land assessment to move-in day, plan for roughly 12–18 months on a standard custom build. The phases break down roughly like this:

  • Land assessment and planning: 1–2 months
  • Builder selection and financing: 1–2 months
  • Permitting and site prep: 1–3 months (varies by county)
  • Construction: 6–12 months depending on home size and complexity

Build in buffer time — weather, supply chain, and permitting delays are common. If you want to be in your home by a specific date, work backward from there.
